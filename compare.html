Decide before you commit

Which AI tool fits your work?

Two questions worth answering before you pick: which Claude product matches what you're trying to do, and whether Claude is the right vendor compared to OpenAI and Google. The landscape moves fast and the right answer depends on your actual workflow, not on whoever shipped the loudest feature last week.

Disclosure: This guide is hosted at claude-cowork — we have an obvious Anthropic slant. We've tried to be honest about where the other vendors win.

The Claude family

Anthropic's products, side by side.

All of these run the same underlying Claude models (currently Opus 4.6, Sonnet 4.6, Haiku 4.5 as of May 2026). The differences are about where Claude lives and what it can touch.

Across vendors

Anthropic vs OpenAI vs Google.

All three ship strong models. The right pick depends on what ecosystem your work lives in, what you need the AI to do, and how much of an enterprise platform you need around it.

Anthropic Claude
"The thoughtful coworker"

Flagship products

Claude.ai, Claude desktop (with Chat / Cowork / Code modes), Anthropic API, Claude in Chrome, Claude for Excel/PowerPoint (beta).

Where it wins

  • Reasoning depth and writing quality
  • Safety and behavior consistency
  • Long context (up to 1M tokens on some models)
  • Coding (well-regarded for refactors and complex bugs)
  • Cowork mode for files + computer use

Where it's behind

  • No first-party image or video generation
  • Smaller voice-mode story
  • No "store" of third-party assistants like GPTs
  • Smaller mainstream consumer mindshare

Plans

Free, Pro, Max ($100/$200), Team ($20–$100/seat), Enterprise. Cowork is included in all paid plans.

Reach for it when

Work is text-heavy, files matter, and you want strong reasoning + agentic action without leaving Anthropic's stack.

OpenAI ChatGPT
"The everything store"

Flagship products

ChatGPT (web, mobile, desktop), GPT-5.5 model (launched April 2026), API, Sora (video), DALL-E/Images 2.0, the GPT Store, custom GPTs, ChatGPT Agents.

Where it wins

  • Broadest ecosystem and feature surface
  • Best image and video generation (Images 2.0 at 2K, Sora)
  • Strongest voice mode
  • Custom GPTs and the GPT Store for shared assistants
  • Agent mode (computer-use, long-running tasks)

Where it's behind

  • Behavior can be less consistent across releases
  • Heavier surface = more learning curve for newcomers
  • Fewer first-party desktop-native tools (no direct Cowork analog)
  • Pricing has shifted upward in 2026

Plans

Free, Go, Plus, Pro, Business, Enterprise. API pricing rose with GPT-5.5: standard $5/$30 per million tokens; Pro tier $30/$180.

Reach for it when

You want one tool for everything — chat, image, voice, video, custom assistants — or you need the broadest third-party ecosystem.

Google Gemini
"Where your work already lives"

Flagship products

Gemini app (web, mobile), Gemini for Workspace (Docs/Gmail/Sheets/Meet), Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform (formerly Vertex AI), NotebookLM, Project Mariner (browsing agent), Workspace Studio.

Where it wins

  • Deepest integration with Google Workspace
  • NotebookLM for research synthesis
  • Strongest enterprise agent platform (200+ models in Model Garden)
  • Aggressive pricing — Gemini 3.5 Flash cheaper than GPT-5.5
  • Bundled into Workspace plans (Business Standard+)

Where it's behind

  • Standalone chat experience feels less polished than ChatGPT
  • Confusing product naming (frequent rebrands — Bard → Gemini → AI Studio → Vertex → now Enterprise Agent Platform)
  • Outside Workspace, less obvious choice
  • Smaller third-party developer ecosystem

Plans

Google AI Plus ($7.99), Pro ($19.99), Ultra ($99.99 — cut from $249.99). Gemini included in Workspace Business Standard, Plus, Enterprise.

Reach for it when

Your work is already in Google Workspace, you want enterprise-scale agent infrastructure, or you need the best price-per-token at scale.

Quick decision tree

If you just want one answer.

A rough sorting hat. Real life is messier — you might end up using two of these together — but if you're starting from zero, here's where to lean.

If you...

Need an AI that does real work on your computer

→ Claude (Cowork mode). Cowork is the most developed desktop-native AI coworker among the three vendors. ChatGPT has agents, but they're cloud-side; Gemini's strongest agent story is enterprise-platform. For "AI on my Mac touching my actual files," Claude is the most direct answer.
If you...

Write code professionally

→ Claude Code — or pair Code with Cursor/GitHub Copilot. Claude is the model most engineers reach for on hard refactors and complex bugs. GPT-5.5 is excellent and OpenAI's Codex spinout is competitive. Google's Code Assist is fine if you're already in their stack.
If you...

Want one tool for chat, images, voice, video, and custom assistants

→ ChatGPT. The widest feature surface in one subscription. Images 2.0, Sora for video, robust voice mode, custom GPTs, the GPT Store. Pay attention to the agent features — they got significantly better in 2026.
If you...

Live in Google Workspace

→ Gemini. If Docs, Gmail, Sheets, and Meet are where you work, having AI built in beats switching tools. Gemini is bundled into Workspace Business Standard and up. Side benefit: NotebookLM is the best research-synthesis tool any vendor ships.
If you...

Are building an enterprise agent platform

→ Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform. Google's 2026 consolidation of Vertex AI into a unified agent platform is the most complete enterprise offering — 200+ models in the Model Garden (including Claude), A2A protocol, partner agents from Box / Workday / Salesforce / ServiceNow.
If you...

Care most about safety, reasoning quality, and long-context work

→ Claude. Claude has the strongest reputation among regulated industries (legal, healthcare, finance) and for tasks that require holding a lot of context (long documents, complex multi-step reasoning).
In practice

Most pros use two.

In reality, very few people commit to one vendor exclusively. The two most common combinations:

Claude (Cowork) + ChatGPT

Claude for the day job — reasoning, files, scheduled tasks, computer use. ChatGPT for image generation, voice conversations, and anything where Custom GPTs save time. Pay $20-30/month for each; total still less than the equivalent in headcount or other software.

Gemini (in Workspace) + Claude

Gemini does the in-app work in Docs / Gmail / Sheets where it's already there. Claude handles everything that needs deeper reasoning, agentic file work, or the Cowork-style scheduled tasks Gemini doesn't match.

Most underrated combo

Claude Cowork + ChatGPT voice mode. Cowork does the heavy lifting on your Mac while ChatGPT's voice mode handles thinking-out-loud conversations during walks or commutes. Different interaction modes, different use cases — they don't compete.
