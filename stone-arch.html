The model

A cooperative, not an agency.

Stone Arch is structured as a co-op of AI practitioners. Revenue flows back to the instructor-members who deliver the work. That matters because the people teaching your cohort are the same people designing the curriculum — not subcontractors briefed in the day before. Our shared knowledge base grows with every engagement, so each new program and each new prospect benefits from accumulated experience instead of starting from scratch.

Status · May 2026

Stone Arch Collective is the brand name for the entity being formalized by June 16, 2026. Final legal structure (LLC, hybrid LLC, or 308B cooperative) is being decided in parallel. All products below are live or in pilot under the Stone Arch banner now.

What we offer

Four product lines.

Each one solves a different version of the same problem: people and organizations need to get fluent with AI fast, and most training off the shelf doesn't fit.

Cohort program

Multi-week, peer-cohort AI education for small businesses, nonprofits, and schools. Built around the way adults actually learn new tools: with peers, with real work to bring to class, with structured time to apply what was just taught. Cohort 1 is in pilot now.

Education Conference

Three-day, in-person, multi-role workshops for K-12 schools. Designed so teachers, administrators, and IT staff leave with shared language and concrete classroom-ready practice. Inaugural conference: August 2026 at Best Buy Corporate Campus.

Pro bono service delivery

One nonprofit and one school per month receive a 10-hour engagement — or comped cohort/conference seats — from Stone Arch. That's 24 organizations per year, roughly 240 hours, approximately $42K of donated value. Mission-aligned giving is part of the model, not a side program.

Advisory & consulting engagements

Private cohort tier for organizations who want their team trained together, and bespoke advisory work for SMB, nonprofit, and school leadership teams navigating AI strategy decisions. Scoped per engagement; pricing on request.

Who we serve

Three audiences with overlapping needs.

We focus on organizations that need substantive AI fluency but are usually skipped over by enterprise-tier training programs — either because of budget, scale, or both.

Small businesses

SMBs that want their team to actually use AI tools well — not just have a license. We meet teams where they are: real workflows, real tools, real time savings.

Nonprofits

Mission-driven organizations that need AI fluency to extend their reach without growing their team. Pro bono delivery is part of how we serve here; advisory engagements are part of how we serve at scale.

K-12 schools

Districts and individual schools building AI competency across teachers, admin, and IT. The Education Conference is purpose-built here; cohort programs serve the same audience throughout the year.
